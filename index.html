ℹ Methodology

Volatility is the annualized standard deviation of daily log returns over the last 252 trading days.

MCTR (Marginal Contribution to Risk) measures how much total portfolio risk changes if you increase the weight of asset i by a tiny amount. MCTR = (Covariance_matrix * weights)_i / Portfolio_Volatility.

% Risk Contribution = (Weight_i * MCTR_i) / Portfolio_Volatility. These sum to 100% and show the proportional risk each asset contributes.

$ Risk Contribution = % Risk Contribution * Total Portfolio Value. This is the dollar amount of risk attributable to each position.

VaR (Value at Risk) at 95% confidence: the expected maximum portfolio loss over one trading day, assuming normal distribution. Historical VaR uses the actual percentile of historical returns.

CVaR / Expected Shortfall: the average loss in the worst (1-confidence)% of cases. More informative than VaR because it captures tail risk.

Beta: covariance of asset with S&P 500 divided by S&P 500 variance. Shows systematic risk relative to the broad market.

Stress Tests apply simultaneous shocks to all holdings based on historical correlation, showing potential portfolio impact.
